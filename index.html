SASS

SASS

Pre-processors in general have been great for CSS and while most have a similar feature set, I have found myself going back to SASS again and again, taking advantage of it's powerful mixins, extends, directives and it's ability to let you write in a nested format without it being compiled into nested CSS. Not having to worry about overly complex specifity, working in nested SASS helps me to visualize and write better modularized code.
